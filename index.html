Performance Factor Analysis

Delivery Rate (96.77%)

The percentage of emails that successfully reach recipients' inboxes (not bounced back)

What Affects It:

  • List Quality: Valid email addresses without typos
  • Spam Trigger Words: Using words like "FREE!", "URGENT!", and excessive caps can trigger spam filters
  • Image-to-Text Ratio: Too many images can trigger spam filters

Open Rate (26.17%)

The percentage of delivered emails that recipients open

What Affects It:

  • Subject Line: The number one factor affecting OR. SLs that follow key points usually outperform those that don't
  • Preheader: Secondary factor, much less important than SL. Should be used to compliment SL and continue to drive the point further
  • Sender Name: Limited data, majority are division names with very few distinct examples
  • Send Time: 7-9 AM, Tuesday - Thursday

Findings: SG users respond best to clear, specific benefits or announcements that relate to their needs. They are also more likely to open emails with personalized content from sources they recognize.

Subject Line Building Guidelines

Includes at least 1 of the following. Sweet spot is using 2 at a time:

  • User specific language (Location, Industry, Role, Name, Company Name)
  • Direct user benefit (dollar amount, feature, problem solution, convenience)
  • Focused branding (Brand name + direct benefit relationship)
  • Urgency/Relevance (Limited time, curiosity builder, user relevance to message - why should they care?)

Examples:

Good Examples

BRUNT. Our newest brand partner has arrived. (23% OR)

Savannah: There's a new way to get your safety shoes. (36% OR)

Less Good Examples

Timberland PRO® just got even better. (17% OR)

Summer work shoe shopping has begun. (9% OR)

Click-Through Rate (CTR) / Click-to-Open Rate (CToR)

What It Means:

CTR: The percentage of delivered emails where recipients clicked on links

CToR: Of people who opened the email, what percentage clicked on something

What Affects It:

Email Content

  • • Expectation Match: Content delivers on subject line/preheader promise
  • • Urgency: Time-sensitive offers or deadlines, upcoming events
  • • Value Proposition: Clear benefit for clicking. Problem user has and solution we are offering
  • • Compelling Copy: Persuasive, benefit-focused writing
  • • Trust Elements: Testimonials, certifications, guarantees

Call-to-Action (CTA)

  • • Buttons: Clear, action-oriented text ("Shop Now", "Get Started")
  • • Multiple Options: Different ways to engage (phone, email, website link)
  • • Clarity: Obvious what happens when clicked

Targeting

  • • Audience Relevance: Knowing the audience and relating the message
  • • List Size: Ideal range is between 2000-5000 contacts. Up to 15,000
  • • Segmentation: Tailored content for specific groups

Design

  • • Spacing: Makes good use of the top half to hook users
  • • Visual Hierarchy: Easy to scan and read (67% of SG users fully read emails)
  • • Mobile Optimization: Works on all devices. ~50% of users prefer mobile

Opt-Out Rate Analysis

What It Means:

The percentage of people who unsubscribe after receiving an email

What Affects It:

The nature of our industry pushes opt outs. Companies constantly rotating employees and locations.

Aside from that, the key factors affecting Opt Outs are:

  • Send Frequency: How many of the same or similar emails we send out, not necessarily how many unique emails we send per period of time
  • Content Relevance: This is supported by the other metrics and by our spam complaints which are extremely low. We have good content; it's just being sent to the wrong people due to poor list management/segmentation

Case Study: Gulfstream Subsidy Announcement

Let's look at an example of an email constructed following the above guidelines.

First, the list is segmented. Broken up by location with size below 5000 contacts per list. There were 12 email versions in total for Gulfstream, and they all performed well.

It all works together, establishing a single point at the top and supporting it with relevant and direct communication, anticipating user need and questions.

The Result

36.3% Open Rate | 46.2% Click Through

That is clearly an outlier, but it does represent the potential numbers we can strive for.

Aside from the layout of the email, one other factor I believe pushes it over the top is the support placed on the campaign. One thing that has become apparent from this review is that the campaigns that are supported prior to and throughout launch by the sales team always outperform those that are "cold launched".

Next Steps

Short Term

  • • Copy & Design Optimization: Adjusting copy and design to provide the key factors the data is supporting and testing the results.
  • • Audience Segmentation: Improve audience segmentation habits during campaign building/brief.
  • • A/B Testing: Implement A/B testing and list separation. For larger campaigns (10k or more sized list) break up the list into parts and test varying versions of the same email by adjusting SL, CTA, sections of copy, images.
  • • Copy Assistant: I've created a virtual (AI) assistant using NotebookLM. It already has all of the basic information for copy and metrics for every relevant email that was reviewed. This will allow copy/design to ask it questions about copies used in the past and how they performed, and it can even offer suggestions. I will be training it with more information as time goes on and updating it regularly.

Long Term

  • • Dynamic Images: Similar to how we can now pull dynamic copy, I've figured out a way to store and call images into sections directly from Salesforce to generate emails in chunks rather than individually. We can create brand packets for headers, footers, etc and generate multiple of the same email, appropriately branded, from one email template.
  • • Consistent Content: Have more consistent email content going out. Starting with the SG general drip revamp and a regular email for new contacts.
